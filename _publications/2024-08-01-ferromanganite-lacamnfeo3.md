## Summary

The double perovskite-type ferro-manganite La₀.₅Ca₀.₅Mn₀.₅Fe₀.₅O₃ was synthesized and characterized to evaluate its combined structural, electrical, optical and magnetic behaviour. The material crystallizes in an orthorhombic perovskite structure (space group Pbnm) and behaves as a semiconductor with an optical band gap of about 2.3 eV, while showing a soft ferromagnetic response below room temperature. Taken together, these features point to a single multifunctional material that couples semiconducting and magnetic behaviour, of interest for spintronic and energy-related applications. This work derives from the author's meritorious M.Sc. (Physics) thesis at the National University of Colombia.
