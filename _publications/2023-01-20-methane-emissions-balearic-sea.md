## Summary

This field study quantifies dissolved methane concentrations and sea–air methane fluxes in the coastal waters of the Balearic Islands (Western Mediterranean), based on measurements at three sites — two near the populated island of Mallorca and one in the protected Cabrera national park — between 2018 and 2021. Methane concentrations ranged from roughly 2.7 to 10.9 nM, with no significant differences among sites, and the estimated fluxes varied seasonally between about 0.2 and 9.7 µmol m⁻² d⁻¹. Over the whole period the three areas behaved as weak net sources of methane to the atmosphere. The work was carried out during the author's research stay at CSIC-IMEDEA (Palma de Mallorca, Spain).
