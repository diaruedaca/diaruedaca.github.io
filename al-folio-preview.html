Diego Alejandro Rueda Cadavid

PhD candidate in Chemical Engineering at UFSC, Brazil · Functional materials & catalysis for green hydrogen.

Environmental Engineer with an M.Sc. in Physics and an M.Sc. in Environmental Sciences, currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering at the Federal University of Santa Catarina (UFSC), Brazil, supported by a CAPES scholarship.

My research focuses on the synthesis and catalytic design of perovskites and double perovskites for hydrogen production and oxygen transport, with strong hands-on experience in the synthesis and characterization of semiconductor and nanostructured materials. I am especially interested in extending this work toward perovskite-based solar cells.
